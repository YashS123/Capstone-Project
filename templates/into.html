Get Started

Type 1 Diabetes

Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune condition where the immune system attacks and destroys insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas.

Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes is the most common form of diabetes. It occurs when the body becomes resistant to insulin or doesn't produce enough insulin.

Gestational Diabetes

Gestational diabetes occurs during pregnancy when the body cannot produce enough insulin to meet the extra needs of pregnancy.

Prevention

Regular physical activity, a healthy diet, maintaining a normal body weight, and avoiding tobacco use can prevent or delay the onset of type 2 diabetes.

Complications

Diabetes can lead to various complications, including heart disease, stroke, kidney disease, nerve damage, and vision loss.

Treatment

Treatment for diabetes includes medication, insulin therapy, lifestyle changes, and monitoring blood sugar levels regularly.
